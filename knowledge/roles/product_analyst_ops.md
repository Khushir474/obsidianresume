STEP 2 — WRITE THE RESUME. Follow every rule below:

- Mirror JD language exactly on first use — if the JD says
  "launch readiness," use those exact words, not "launch preparation"
- Jira must appear in: Technical Skills AND at least two Work
  Experience bullets — it is the highest-weighted tool keyword
  for this role
- If the JD mentions Jira Product Discovery (JPD), treat it as
  a separate keyword from Jira and include both
- Spell out on first use: "Product Development Lifecycle (PDLC),"
  "Go-To-Market (GTM)," "Key Performance Indicator (KPI),"
  "Objectives and Key Results (OKRs)," "Standard Operating
  Procedure (SOP)" — abbreviate after
- Every hard keyword from List A must appear in BOTH the Skills
  section AND at least one Work Experience bullet
- Every soft keyword from List B must be woven naturally into
  at least one bullet
- Every process term from List C must appear in at least one bullet
- Name specific teams in cross-functional bullets — not
  "cross-functional teams" but "Product, Engineering, and
  Customer Success teams"
- Dates must be written as: "June 2025 – Present" — no apostrophes,
  no abbreviations
- Single-column format only — no tables, columns, text boxes, icons

PROCESS LANGUAGE RULE:
This role is inherently process-driven. Unlike DS or ML Engineer
resumes, operational verbs score well here with both ATS and humans:
"managed," "coordinated," "maintained," "monitored," "reported,"
"escalated," "documented," "tracked," "aligned."
Use these where accurate — do not suppress them for style reasons.
Reserve Paul Graham verbs ("cut," "built," "shipped") for
high-impact bullets only.

EXTRAPOLATION:
Fill the gap between what I wrote and what a strong product ops
practitioner would have done. If I coordinated teams, assume I ran
structured status cadences, maintained a source of truth in Jira,
and escalated blockers proactively. If I built dashboards, assume
I defined the reporting framework, QA'd the data, and presented
weekly to leadership. If I tracked metrics, assume I built the
measurement framework before tracking anything. If I managed a
product or feature, assume the full PDLC arc: discovery → PRD →
build → release → adoption — show the role played in each phase.
Push to what I can defend in an interview.

WRITING STYLE — Paul Graham (human layer, applied after ATS):
- Lead with ownership — never "supported" or "assisted"
- Name the teams you worked with — specificity signals credibility
- Every bullet answers: what did you own, and what changed
  because of it
- No corporate filler: "cross-functional synergies,"
  "stakeholder alignment initiatives," "leveraged best practices"
- Plain language a non-technical hiring manager reads in five seconds
