STEP 1 — BEFORE WRITING ANYTHING:
Extract three lists from the JD:
A) Hard keywords: every tool, language, platform, and methodology
   named (e.g. "SQL," "Python," "Tableau," "dbt," "Snowflake,"
   "A/B testing," "cohort analysis")
B) Soft keywords: every trait or competency named (e.g.
   "stakeholder," "cross-functional," "self-starter,"
   "detail-oriented")
C) Business domain terms: every business metric or outcome named
   (e.g. "revenue," "churn," "conversion," "retention," "pipeline")
All items from all three lists must appear in the resume.
Show me Lists A, B, and C before writing.

STEP 2 — WRITE THE RESUME. Follow every rule below:

ATS RULES (non-negotiable):
- Mirror JD language exactly on first use — if the JD says
  "cohort analysis," use those exact words, not "segmentation"
- Identify the exact BI tool named in the JD (Tableau, Looker,
  Power BI, Metabase) and use only that tool name — not the
  whole family
- Spell out every acronym on first use: "Exploratory Data Analysis
  (EDA)," "Key Performance Indicator (KPI)," "Month-over-Month
  (MoM)," "Year-over-Year (YoY)" — abbreviate after
- SQL must appear in: Technical Skills AND at least three separate
  Work Experience bullets — it is the highest-weighted keyword for
  this role
- Every hard keyword from List A must appear in BOTH the Skills
  section AND at least one Work Experience bullet
- Every soft keyword from List B must be woven naturally into
  at least one bullet
- Every business domain term from List C must appear in at least
  one bullet
- Dates must be written as: "June 2025 – Present" — no apostrophes,
  no abbreviations
- Single-column format only — no tables, columns, text boxes, icons

ROLE-SPECIFIC ATS SIGNALS TO HIT:
- SQL: complex SQL queries, joins, window functions, aggregations, query optimization
- Analysis: exploratory data analysis (EDA), ad hoc analysis, cohort analysis, funnel analysis, hypothesis testing
- Business: revenue impact, conversion rate, customer retention, churn, MoM growth, YoY growth
- Dashboard: self-service analytics, dashboard adoption, stakeholder reporting, data visualization
- Stack: dbt, Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift, Databricks, Amplitude, Mixpanel — use whichever the JD names

BULLET STRUCTURE RULE:
Every bullet must contain BOTH the technical method AND the business
outcome — not one or the other. ATS needs the method for keyword
scoring; humans need the outcome for relevance.

Right: "Wrote complex SQL queries on Databricks to analyze 750K+
customer records, surfacing conversion rate gaps that informed
Sephora's Q3 acquisition strategy"

Wrong (ATS fails): "Surfaced insights that drove Sephora's
acquisition strategy" — SQL invisible, keyword score drops

Wrong (human fails): "Wrote SQL queries in Databricks" —
no business context, recruiter skips it

EXTRAPOLATION:
Fill the gap between what I wrote and what a strong analyst would
have done. If I built dashboards, assume the full ownership arc:
defined the metrics, built it, drove adoption, maintained it —
not just "built a dashboard." If I ran analysis, assume I documented
methodology and presented findings with a recommendation, not just
a chart. Push to what I can defend in an interview.

WRITING STYLE — Paul Graham (human layer, applied after ATS):
- Frame around business outcome — but keep the technical method
  visible in the same bullet
- Plain language — if a non-technical hiring manager can't follow
  it in five seconds, rewrite it
- "Stakeholder" must appear — it is the most important soft keyword
  for this role type
- No jargon stacking, no passive voice
- No: leveraged, spearheaded, utilized, streamlined

FORMAT:
Technical Skills — grouped by: Languages | Analytics & BI |
                   Data Platforms | Statistical Methods | Tools
                   All List A keywords present, full names +
                   abbreviations both included
Work Experience  — 3-4 bullets per role, method + outcome in each,
                   dates as "Month YYYY – Month YYYY"
Education        — Degree, Major, Institution, coursework for University of Chicago, Graduation Year

At the bottom, add:
"EXTRAPOLATED — PREP BEFORE INTERVIEW"
For each addition: what was added | what it assumes | one sentence
on how to talk about it honestly
